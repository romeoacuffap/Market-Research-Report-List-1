<p><ul><li>Double Layer</li><li>Pseudocapacitor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The liquid electrolyte supercapacitor market comprises two main types: electric double-layer capacitors (EDLCs) and pseudocapacitors. EDLCs store energy through the electrostatic separation of charge at the interface between the electrode and electrolyte, offering high capacitance and rapid charge/discharge capabilities. In contrast, pseudocapacitors utilize fast redox reactions to achieve higher energy density, combining features of traditional capacitors and batteries. Both types serve diverse applications, including energy storage in electronics and renewable energy systems, driven by their unique performance characteristics.</p></p>
