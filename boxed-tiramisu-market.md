<p><p>The boxed tiramisu market primarily includes two types of bases: ladyfingers and sponge cake. Ladyfingers, or savoiardi, are traditional in tiramisu, offering a light, textured bite that absorbs coffee well without becoming overly soggy. Sponge cake provides a softer, denser alternative, enhancing the dessert's creaminess. Both product types cater to varying consumer preferences, with ladyfingers appealing to purists and sponge cake attracting those seeking a richer mouthfeel. This diversity enables manufacturers to meet diverse market demands effectively.</p></p>
