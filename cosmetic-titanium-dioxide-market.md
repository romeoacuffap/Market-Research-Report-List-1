<p><ul><li>Chloride Process</li><li>Sulfate Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cosmetic titanium dioxide market primarily utilizes two production processes: the chloride process and the sulfate process. The chloride process involves reacting titanium raw materials with chlorine at high temperatures, resulting in a purer and more refined titanium dioxide, favored for high-quality cosmetics. Conversely, the sulfate process uses sulfuric acid to extract titanium, leading to a more cost-effective but less pure product, often utilized in budget-friendly cosmetics. Each process caters to different market segments based on quality and price preferences.</p></p>
