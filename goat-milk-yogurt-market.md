<p><p>The goat milk yogurt market features two main types: set-style yogurt and Swiss-style yogurt. Set-style yogurt is produced by heating the milk, cooling it, and then adding live cultures, resulting in a thicker, creamier texture as it is set directly in containers. In contrast, Swiss-style yogurt undergoes a blending process after fermentation, creating a smoother, more pourable consistency, often with added fruit or flavors. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences, offering unique taste experiences and health benefits associated with goat milk.</p></p>
